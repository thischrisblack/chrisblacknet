This Is Chris Black

I'm a software developer at the American Board of Radiology, one of the 24 national boards tasked with setting professional standards for medical specialty practice and certification, where Covid-19 has us at hard at work making our normally in-person processes fully-remote!

I love JavaScript and everything around it.

Selected Projects

I work professionally in TypeScript, Aurelia, Node.js, and SQL, but I like to take opportunities in my personal projects to explore other parts of the JavaScript universe.

Shelly Black's Website

  • React
  • TypeScript
  • Netlify

The website/blog of photographer and archivist Shelly Black (Borat voice: my wife). In an effort to stay relevent, and also because it's a pleasure to work with, I chose React, and also gave Netlify a try for the CMS and deployment.

  • Live site: www.shelly-black.com
  • Code: github.com

Emoji Disco

  • JavaScript
  • Web Audio API
  • HTML
  • CSS

The first of what will likely be many experiments with the Web Audio API. Upload a music file to get the party started! The dB levels of selected frequency bands animate the dancers, the lights, the DJ, the turntables, and the speakers.

  • Live site: thischrisblack.github.io
  • Code: github.com

Glenngary Bob Ross

  • Node.js
  • JIMP
  • Heroku

A Twitterbot that tweets dynamically-created images of Bob Ross captioned with random lines from Blake's monologue in Glengarry Glen Ross. It is a humorous juxtaposition.

  • Live site: twitter.com/glengarrybob
  • Code: github.com

Tailwind Config Color Spectrum

  • JavaScript
  • Tailwind CSS

This one is niche, but we use Tailwind CSS at work with a ton of custom colors and I got tired of guessing which one "gray-300" might be. Taste the rainbow! (This is a blunt tool so your mileage may vary.)

  • Live site: thischrisblack.github.io
  • Code: github.com

About this site

The previous incarnation was a React/Gatsby site deployed with Firebase [GitHub repo], then one day I just lost my mind and said "I want to make a website with just HTML, CSS, and JavaScript" and here we are. The repo is here (or you could just view source). I had fun experimenting with Web Components for this one (for the logo and music players).
